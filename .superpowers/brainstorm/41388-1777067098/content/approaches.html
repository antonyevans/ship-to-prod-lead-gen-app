Three landing page approaches

Each handles the same sections differently. Pick the direction that feels right.

A

Narrative Scroll

Classic SaaS flow: Hero → Problem → How it works → Screenshot → Comparison → Social proof → Pricing → CTA. Familiar structure, easy to scan. Good for SEO. Slightly generic for a brand this opinionated.

Pros

  • Converts well — proven pattern
  • Easy to build fast
  • Works on mobile

Cons

  • Doesn't feel like Surfaced
  • Looks like every other tool
B

Evidence-First ⭐ Recommended

Opens with a live-looking pain signal example — the "receipt" — before explaining the product. Hero shows the output, not the pitch. Rest of page follows naturally. Most on-brand with "Find the wound. Open with the receipt."

Pros

  • Instantly shows value, not claims
  • Embodies brand position
  • Memorable — stands out in feed

Cons

  • Slightly higher design complexity
  • Needs convincing synthetic signal
C

Manifesto Page

Sparse, copy-heavy, opinionated. Opens with a short essay on why cold outreach is broken. Product is almost secondary — the point of view does the selling. More like a Stripe or Linear launch page.

Pros

  • Distinctive — builds brand memory
  • ICP will feel seen immediately

Cons

  • Harder to convert cold traffic
  • Risky if copy isn't perfect
  • Less scannable
