# Surfaced — Voice DNA

## Identity & Purpose
Surfaced is an AI outbound pipeline for founders of AI service companies who need leads but not another tool to manage. We find a specific, public reason to call each prospect before any contact is made — a bad review, a broken booking page, missed after-hours calls — then open every call with that evidence verbatim. We exist because outreach should start with a reason, not a pitch.

## Archetype Foundation
Primary archetype: Magician — core desire: transformation. The pipeline runs on screen, two fields become a completed call, the complex appears effortless. Voice implication: state what happened, not how. The result is the story.
Secondary archetype: Sage — what edge it adds: intellectual credibility. We don't just run the pipeline, we know why it works. Voice implication: confident claims grounded in specific evidence, never in generalisation.

2-word filter: Effortless · Deliberate — every piece of copy should feel like both words. If it embodies only one, revise. Effortless without deliberate = glib. Deliberate without effortless = heavy.

## Tone Parameters
- Casual/Formal: 50/100
- Playful/Serious: 55/100
- Enthusiastic/Matter-of-fact: 30/100
- Irreverent/Respectful: 65/100
- Simple/Technical: 30/100

Emotional register: quiet authority. Not excitement, not urgency. The specific emotion this brand evokes is the feeling of walking into a meeting already knowing what the other person doesn't know yet.

## Voice Patterns — Statistical
- Average sentence length: 10–14 words
- Fragment frequency: occasional — used for emphasis and rhythm, never for padding
- Question frequency: rare — one per piece of content maximum. The Knowing Advisor states; it doesn't ask.
- First-person: "we" in brand copy, "Surfaced" in product/UI copy
- Signature openings: lead with the finding or the contradiction. Never lead with "we" followed by a claim about ourselves.
- Signature closings: end on the brand position or the specific payoff. Never end on a vague CTA.

## Personality Decomposition

Knowing: sounds like "You already know cold calls without a reason get ignored. We just fixed that." — does NOT sound like citing a statistic to prove a point the reader already accepts.

Wry: sounds like "Apparently 'AI-powered' now means 'we imported your CSV and added a subject line.'" — does NOT sound like mean-spirited competitor attacks. The wryness is about the category, not a specific rival.

Measured: sounds like "One signal. One call. One reason." — does NOT sound like enthusiasm or exclamation marks. The Knowing Advisor never uses (!).

Authoritative: sounds like "The call shouldn't start with a pitch. It should start with the evidence." — does NOT sound like hedging or qualifying.

## Vocabulary System

Always say: signal, before, found, evidence, reason, specific, real, looked, qualified, open, wound, receipt, surface (verb), verified, live, public, already

Never say: leverage, seamless, streamlined, revolutionary, optimise, robust, scalable, game-changing, innovative, synergy, personalised (as a feature claim), AI-powered (as a marketing claim), solution, unlock, elevate, delve, "let's dive in", "at the end of the day", world-class, curated, empower, transform, disrupt, paradigm

Brand secret language:
- "the wound" — the specific pain signal found in public data
- "the receipt" — the evidence used to open a call
- "signal-qualified" — a prospect with a live, verifiable pain signal
- "before we dial" — the moment of research
- "we looked" — the brand behaviour in two words

Preferred alternatives:
- "personalised email" → relevant call
- "AI-powered" → we actually read the reviews
- "pipeline" → run
- "outreach campaign" → call
- "ICP" → who you're selling to
- "solution" → fix
- "prospects" → businesses

## Channel Applications

Product descriptions: 2–4 sentences. Lead with what was found. End with the time or simplicity payoff. No CTA.

Email: Subject line states a finding, not a benefit. Opening names the specific signal first. Max 5 sentences. No pleasantries.

Social (LinkedIn): Open with irony or contradiction. 3 short paragraphs max. No hashtags. No "thoughts?". Last line carries the brand position.

Social (X): Under 220 characters. Single observation as fact. Dry. No exclamation marks.

UI / microcopy: State what happened. "Surfaced found 3 signals." Not "Great news! We found some signals for you!"

Customer service: Name the specific issue before offering a fix. Never "I understand your frustration."

## Anti-Patterns

1. Over-explaining the mechanism — state the result, not how the detection engine works.
2. Wry becoming smug — wryness is about the category, never about making the reader feel slow.
3. Measured becoming flat — short sentences with rhythm prevent energy loss.
4. Sage overcorrecting into explanation — lead with findings, not rationale.

## Golden Examples Library

"Surfaced found 3 signals. Calling prospect #1 now."
→ States fact, implies work done, moves forward. No fanfare.

"You don't need to research them. We already did."
→ 8 words, addresses exact problem, positions brand in second sentence.

"Surfaced finds a specific, public reason to call each prospect — a bad review, a broken booking page, a gap in their after-hours coverage — then opens the call with that evidence verbatim."
→ Concrete examples, "verbatim" signals precision, no feature list.

"You've built an AI product that handles calls for other businesses. Your outbound is still 'post on LinkedIn and hope.' That's the irony we fix."
→ Names the situation, quotes their behaviour, lands position in 5 words.

"Apparently 'AI-powered' now means 'we imported your CSV and added a subject line.'"
→ Calls out the category without naming a competitor.

"Call with a receipt." — 4 words. The entire brand position.

## Calibration Test
1. Does it feel Effortless AND Deliberate?
2. Does it contain any banned words or AI tells?
3. Does it lead with a specific finding or concrete claim?
4. Does it have a clear point of view, stated without hedging?
5. Would a reader recognise this as the same voice as the Golden Examples?
