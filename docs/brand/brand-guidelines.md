# Surfaced — Brand Guidelines

## 1. Brand Foundation

**Purpose**
The cold call is broken because it starts with a pitch, not a problem. Surfaced exists to flip that: find the evidence first, then make contact. Every outreach should open with something the prospect can verify.

**Vision**
A world where sales calls start with a reason — where AI closes the gap between "someone might need this" and "we know exactly why they need this, today."

**Mission**
Surface the specific, public evidence that a business is struggling — then put that evidence in the opening line of every call.

**Values**
1. Evidence-first — no call without a verifiable reason to make it
2. Specificity over scale — one relevant signal beats a thousand generic touches
3. Respect for time — the prospect's and yours
4. Visible pipeline — no black boxes; the work runs on screen
5. Speed — research that takes humans days should take seconds

**Brand Essence**
"Find the wound. Open with the receipt."

---

## 2. Target Customer

AI service founders (1–10 people) building voice agents, automation tools, or chatbots. Pre-revenue to ~$500K ARR. They automate other people's workflows but haven't automated their own outbound. Their pipeline is "whoever responded to a LinkedIn DM."

**JTBD:** "When I've built an AI service I believe in but have no consistent way to find buyers, I want to reach prospects who already have the specific problem I solve — so I can open with evidence, not a pitch."

**Key pain points (in their language):**
- "I know exactly who should be buying this. I just have no way to reach them at scale without it feeling gross."
- "Every outbound tool I've tried just blasts emails that get ignored."
- "Our best clients came from someone introducing us. I can't build a company on warm intros."
- "I need to be building the product, not spending a Sunday researching which businesses might have a booking problem."

---

## 3. Positioning

**Statement**
"For AI service founders who need outbound that converts, Surfaced is the outreach pipeline that opens every call with verifiable evidence of the prospect's pain — unlike Clay or Apollo, where personalisation means merge fields, not proof."

**Golden Circle**
- WHY: Sales calls should start with a reason, not a pitch
- HOW: Real-time pain signal detection from public data, turned into the opening line
- WHAT: An AI pipeline that finds the wound, writes the script, and places the call

**Value Proposition**
- Feature: Real-time pain signal detection from public reviews and websites
- Benefit: Every call opens with something the prospect can independently verify
- Emotional: You show up informed, not desperate

**Brand Pillars:** Evidence · Specificity · Speed

---

## 4. Personality

**Archetype:** Magician (70%) + Sage (30%)
**Aaker dimension:** Competence — reliable, intelligent, successful
**Voice:** The Knowing Advisor — Knowing · Wry · Measured · Authoritative
**2-word filter:** Effortless · Deliberate

Stands for: evidence before contact · specific over scalable · saying what others won't about cold outreach
Stands against: merge-field "personalisation" · volume as a strategy · sounding like every other SDR tool

---

## 5. Logo System

**Direction:** Clean sans wordmark in Plus Jakarta Sans, weight 700. Lowercase preferred. Flare Orange dot (·) used as a signal mark — placed after the wordmark or as a standalone icon.

**Don'ts:** No gradients on the wordmark. No outline versions. No stacked tagline lockup at small sizes.

*Note: Actual logo design requires a designer. This establishes direction.*

## 6. Colour Palette

| Role | Name | HEX | RGB |
|------|------|-----|-----|
| Primary | Charcoal | #1C1C1E | 28, 28, 30 |
| Background | Warm White | #FAFAF7 | 250, 250, 247 |
| Secondary | Sand | #E8E0D4 | 232, 224, 212 |
| Accent | Flare Orange | #FF5C2B | 255, 92, 43 |
| Neutral | Stone | #9A8E82 | 154, 142, 130 |

**Usage:** Charcoal on Warm White is the primary text combination. Flare Orange is used sparingly — signal moments only (a found pain signal, a live call status, a CTA). Sand is the secondary surface. Stone is for supporting text and metadata.

**WCAG:** Charcoal on Warm White passes AA. Flare Orange on Warm White passes AA for large text (18pt+); verify at smaller sizes.

---

## 7. Typography

**Headline:** Plus Jakarta Sans — weight 700/800
**Body:** Inter — weight 400/500

**Rules:**
- Headlines: sentence case, never all-caps
- No italic in UI; italic reserved for quotes in brand materials
- Minimum body size: 14px

---

## 8. Imagery & Photography

**Direction:** Warm Precision — warm directional light, decisive human moments, analog textures alongside digital interfaces.

**Shoot/select:** Single subjects in clear focus, warm uncluttered backgrounds, moments of discovery or confirmation, phone calls and notepads, warm tones (amber, sand).

**Avoid:** Stock handshakes, dark moody shots, abstract AI imagery (neural networks, glowing nodes), groups of people.

---

## 9. Voice & Tone

Full detail: `.brand/voice-dna.md`

**In one line:** The Knowing Advisor — states findings with quiet authority, never explains the trick, occasionally wry about the industry.

**Core rules:**
- Lead with the finding, not the feature
- No exclamation marks
- One rhetorical question per piece, maximum
- Fragments permitted; padding is not
- End on the brand position, not a vague CTA

**Banned words:** leverage, seamless, streamlined, revolutionary, optimise, robust, scalable, game-changing, innovative, synergy, AI-powered (as a claim), solution, unlock, elevate, delve, world-class, curated, empower, transform
